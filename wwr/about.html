History

White water rafting, a thrilling water-based activity, traces its roots to indigenous cultures using primitive watercraft. In the mid-20th century, the advent of inflatable rubber rafts marked the beginning of modern white water rafting, initially with surplus military rafts. The 1970s and 1980s witnessed commercialization, as outfitters emerged, making this exhilarating sport more accessible to the public. It rapidly gained global popularity, with renowned destinations like the Colorado River and Zambezi River. Advancements in equipment and safety measures followed, leading to the classification of rivers by difficulty. Competitive rafting took shape in the 1970s, introducing organized events. Today, white water rafting stands as a widely enjoyed adventure sport, combining excitement, teamwork, and a connection with nature, with continued emphasis on safety and standards.
